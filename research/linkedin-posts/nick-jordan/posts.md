## Post 2 — 2025 Web Almanac: AI Search Responses Are All Wrong

Went through the 2025 Web Almanac data. What I'm seeing is a lot of moves but not a lot of big brain moves.

The good news: the industry is responding to AI search.
The bad news: most of the response is wishful robots.txt entries and unproven config files.

- llms.txt adoption went from 2% of sites in Jul 25 to 5%+ in Jan 26
- Sites adding robots.txt rules for GPTBot up ~55% year over year
- ClaudeBot robots.txt entries nearly doubled
- FAQPage schema climbing again

^ all of this takes about 10 mins. None of it improves your actual CONTENT or makes it worth citing.

llms.txt specifically… SE Ranking analyzed 300,000 domains. No measurable link between having the file and getting cited by LLMs. Their predictive model actually got more accurate when they removed it as a variable.

We've been saying this. llms.txt doesn't work. Google hasn't indicated it's a signal. OpenAI's docs don't mention it affecting citations. The industry adopted it anyway because nobody checked.

Every site we've gotten cited in AI Mode had the same thing in common: good content, structured clearly. Not a single .txt file in the mix.

## Post 4 — SME Interviews Beat 3,000-Word Blog Posts for LLM Citations

A 60-second convo with a subject matter expert is worth more to an LLM than a 3,000-word blog post rewritten from Google's page 1 — and I'll PROVE IT in 1 min.

We have a client in the wealth management niche. High-ticket. Buyers doing months of research before talking to sales.

The advisors at this company knew things nobody had ever written down: specific objections, edge cases, questions that pop up on every call.

None of it existed anywhere on the internet.

We collected ALL those convos, turned it into content and published it under the advisors' names…

THREE of our articles got pulled into Google's AI Overview.
THREE DEMOS followed. Average deal size in the five to six figures.

*(Ofc attribution isn't 100% clean. Nobody's is rn. But we owned the exact query the demos came from on organic and AIO. Coincidence? I think not.)*

Point is: AI cited it because there was nothing else like it anywhere.

The stuff sitting in your sales team's heads, your support tickets, your founder's war stories… that's proprietary data.

And right now most of it is stuck in Slack threads and call recordings where no AI model will ever find it.

Pity.
