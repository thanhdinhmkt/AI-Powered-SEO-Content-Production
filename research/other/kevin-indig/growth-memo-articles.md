## Growth Memo Issue — "How Much Can We Influence AI Responses?"

**URL:** https://www.growth-memo.com/p/how-much-can-we-influence-ai-responses
**Published:** January 2026

**Core argument:**
LLM outputs are probabilistic, not deterministic. This creates a fundamental limit on how reliably any content strategy can guarantee AI citation. Seven reasons why:

1. Lottery-style outputs — same query, different answer each time
2. Context window sensitivity — position of content in the retrieval window affects outcome
3. Temperature settings — LLM creativity settings affect consistency
4. Training data recency — models trained months ago may not weight new content
5. Query phrasing sensitivity — small wording changes produce different citations
6. Model-specific vulnerabilities — what works in ChatGPT may not in Gemini
7. LLM arms race — as more content optimizes for AI, the signal becomes noise

**E-GEO Testbed research referenced:**
- Dataset: 7,000+ real product queries (sourced from Reddit) + 50,000+ Amazon product listings
- "Universal Strategy" finding: longer descriptions with **persuasive tone and deliberate fluff** achieved a 90% win rate vs. baseline
- Cross-category transfer: strategy developed on home goods achieved 87–88% win rate on electronics and clothing
- Conclusion: LLM visibility is highly manipulable — and therefore fragile

**Kevin's take:**
The growing body of research shows extreme fragility in LLM visibility signals. This is an argument for brand-level trust-building over tactical optimization — aligns with Eli Schwartz's framework.
