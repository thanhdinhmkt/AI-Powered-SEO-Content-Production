## Article 1 — AI Search: Where Are We & What Can We Do About It?

**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/search-engine-optimization/ai-search-trends/
**Published:** May 18, 2025
**Category:** Search Engine Optimization / AI Search

**Summary:**
Presented at HiveMCR in Manchester. Provides a data-grounded reality check on AI search's actual impact vs. the hype.

**Key data points extracted:**
- AI traffic currently represents only **1–2% of referral traffic** for most websites
- Revenue potential from AI search is "trivial compared to traditional search" as of mid-2025
- **98.1%** of ChatGPT desktop visitors are shared with Google — only 1.9% exclusive to ChatGPT
- **98.2%** of Perplexity visitors are also Google users
- ChatGPT total visits: ~5.14B vs Google: ~81.31B (April 2025)
- AI Overviews primarily trigger for **informational queries** — not commercial or transactional
- AIO presence *harms* CTR and traffic even for sites *featured* within them

**Forward-looking framework (Aleyda's 2030 scenario):**
By 2030, SEOs will track brand visibility, citations, and revenue across LLMs and search platforms — reframing SEOs as "findability specialists" rather than Google-rank specialists.

**Implications for AI content production:**
Producing AI content targeting informational queries may face accelerating headwinds as AIO absorbs that traffic. Commercial and transactional content retains better organic click share.

## Article 2 — SEO vs GEO: Optimizing for Traditional vs AI Search

**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/search-engine-optimization/seo-vs-geo-optimizing-for-traditional-vs-ai-search/
**Published:** June 2, 2025 (continuously updated)
**Category:** AI Search / Search Engine Optimization

**Summary:**
A structured comparison framework across four dimensions — the most comprehensive public mapping of SEO vs GEO overlap and divergence.

**Four comparison dimensions:**
1. **User Search Behavior** — Traditional: short, keyword-based, one-off queries with high navigational intent. AI: conversational, multi-turn, task-oriented
2. **Optimization Areas** — Traditional: crawlability, keywords, links, structured data. AI: entity clarity, citation-worthiness, structured answers, brand mentions
3. **Results Presentation & Delivery** — Traditional: ranked blue links, SERP features. AI: synthesized answers, citations, follow-up prompts
4. **Goals and Metrics** — Traditional: clicks, rankings, impressions. AI: brand visibility, citation rate, answer presence

**Core thesis:**
The optimization areas have significant overlap at the foundation (technical hygiene, structured data, content clarity) but diverge at the top of the stack. SEO done right is the prerequisite for GEO — not a separate discipline.

## Article 3 — Google AI Mode's Query Fan-Out Technique

**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/ai-search/google-query-fan-out/
**Published:** May 25, 2025
**Category:** AI Search

**Summary:**
Explains Google's newly launched AI Mode (officially launched at Google I/O, May 2025) and its "Query Fan-Out" mechanism.

**What Query Fan-Out means:**
When a user submits a query, Google AI Mode doesn't retrieve results for that single query. Instead, it generates **multiple sub-queries** (the "fan-out"), retrieves results for each, and synthesizes a combined answer.

**SEO implication:**
A single page can be cited for queries it wasn't explicitly optimized for — if it covers related sub-topics that appear in the fan-out. Topical depth and semantic coverage matter more than exact-match keyword targeting.

**Technical note:**
AI Mode uses a custom version of Gemini 2.5 as of May 2025.

## Article 4 — Search Isn't Just Turning to AI, It's Being Re-Monetized

**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/search-engine-optimization/serp-shifts-ads-remonetized/
**Published:** February 14, 2026
**Category:** Search Engine Optimization

**Summary:**
Original data analysis using Similarweb across four verticals (headphones, jeans, online games, greeting cards) comparing January 2025 vs. January 2026 click-share distribution.

**Headphones vertical — top findings:**
- Classic organic click share: **73% → 50%** (−23pp in 12 months)
- Text Ads: **3% → 16%** (+13pp)
- PLAs: **13% → 20%** (+7pp)
- Combined paid (text + PLAs): **16% → 36%** — more than doubled
- AIO presence on SERPs: **2.28% → 32.76%**
- Total click volume from top 5K queries declined: **2.5M → 1.8M**

**Winner/loser patterns:**
- **YouTube: #2 → #1** (178K → 244K organic clicks) — only major site to *grow* absolute organic clicks YoY
- CNET and NYTimes disappeared from the top 10 entirely
- Beatsbydre collapsed from #5 (117K) to #9 (31K)
- Amazon and Walmart moved up in organic rank only because others fell harder — both lost absolute volume

**Paid search response:**
- Amazon: +35% paid clicks YoY
- Walmart: **6x increase** in paid clicks despite not being in organic top 10
- Bose: +49% paid clicks

**Strategic implication for AI SEO content:**
As AIO absorbs informational queries and paid ads expand into commercial queries, pure content-farm strategies face structural compression from both ends. Multi-format content (YouTube + text) becomes the only resilient play.

## Article 5 — What We Learned from Google Search Central Zurich 2025

**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/search-engine-optimization/what-we-learned-google-search-central-zurich-2025/
**Published:** December 10, 2025
**Category:** Search Engine Optimization

**Summary:**
Direct notes from the full-day Google Search Central event in Zurich, with Q&A from John Mueller and Martin Splitt.

**Key Google clarifications extracted:**

1. **"There is no GEO without SEO."** Google explicitly stated at the event that AI search optimization cannot be done without SEO fundamentals. Technical hygiene, structured data, and clear content remain the prerequisite.

2. **llms.txt does not matter much.** Google: "It's just a text file for us." They see it but don't treat it as a meaningful signal.

3. **Schema.org remains important** — especially for data with authoritative meaning (prices in shopping, regulatory information).

4. **AI Overview / AI Mode tracking:** Google confirmed no separate tracking announcement planned. Data is "already included in what's shown in Google Search Console" — which the SEO community disputed.

5. **Google's AI launch decision framework:** Is it good for users? How do you measure "good"? Same principles apply to AI — they're "still measuring and optimizing for user satisfaction."

6. **AI drives more diverse search:** "With AI, people tend to search more and get insights from other types of platforms, like social ones."

## Article 6 — 5 AI Insights from Google Search Central Live in Madrid (April 2025)

**URL:** https://www.aleydasolis.com/en/search-engine-optimization/ai-insights-google-search-central-live/
**Published:** April 10, 2025
**Category:** Search Engine Optimization

**5 Key Takeaways:**

1. **Google's goal and mission don't change** — even as presentation format evolves with AI
2. **AI-generated content** — treated the same as human content; quality is what matters
3. **How AI Overviews work** — optimizing for them is largely the same as optimizing for featured snippets (clear, structured, authoritative answers)
4. **AIO and Gemini tracking** — limited data available; Google has not provided dedicated GSC metrics for AI traffic
5. **Future of SEO with LLMs** — Google's view: SEO fundamentals remain valid; the interface changes but the underlying relevance signals do not
